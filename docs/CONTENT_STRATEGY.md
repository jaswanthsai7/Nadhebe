# CONTENT STRATEGY

## Nadhebe Content Cluster Strategy

This document establishes the publishing guidelines, category directories, technical writing quality metrics, and visual standards for creating articles on Nadhebe.

---

## 1. Content Generation Engine & Categories

Nadhebe translates complex technical concepts and YouTube videos into structured content hubs. Rather than summarizing transcripts, content strategists perform independent, official research.

### Available Directories & Intents
* `/news/`: Breaking news, models releases, and roadmaps.
* `/tutorials/`: Code-based walkthroughs and installation steps.
* `/guides/`: Conceptual frameworks, architecture diagrams, and system designs.
* `/comparisons/`: Head-to-head model or framework benchmarks and tables.
* `/reviews/`: Detailed ratings, pros, cons, and pricing reviews.
* `/tools/`: Feature overviews, compilers, and tool specifications.
* `/frameworks/`: Structural libraries, plugins, and architectures (e.g. SQLite agent states).
* `/prompts/`: System prompt layouts and instruction strategies.
* `/best-practices/`: Design standards, token control, and cost management.
* `/case-studies/`: Business savings, migrations, and performance statistics.
* `/use-cases/`: Agency deployments, corporate setups, and industry use-cases.

---

## 2. Technical Quality Standards

We do not publish filler or generic descriptions. Every article must meet these depth requirements:

* **Analytical Depth**: Explain the *Why*, *How*, and the associated *Trade-offs* or *Limitations*.
* **Code Blocks**: Provide complete, copy-pasteable, and syntactically correct code samples with language descriptors.
* **Mermaid Diagrams**: Include flowcharts, sequence diagrams, and architecture graphs to explain data flows.
* **Tables**: Use comparison tables to summarize metrics and benchmarks.
* **Checklists**: Include operational checklists for deployment or setup.

---

## 3. Formatting & Editorial Standards

* **Author Profiles**: Every article must link to a valid registered author (`alice-chen` or `bob-smith`).
* **Source Listings**: Include a list of authoritative references (GitHub, official docs, research papers) in the frontmatter `sources` block.
* **FAQs**: Include a minimum of two clear, structured FAQs at the bottom of the article to capture question-based search queries.
* **Internal Links**: Actively link related topics, tag pages, and category indexes to build search authority.

---

## 4. Visual Editorial Guidelines

All articles require visual illustrations to improve reading engagement. Images should resemble premium developer documentation illustrations.

### Visual Constraints
* **Style**: Premium, minimal, magazine-quality editorial designs.
* **Atmosphere**: Natural daylight, soft casting shadows, and clean margins.
* **Backgrounds**: Pure white or extremely light grey. **Never use black backgrounds, neon, cyberpunk, or dark theme layouts.**
* **Accents**: Light blue gradients, purple highlights, cyan, or mint tones.
* **Composition**: 16:9 widescreen layout with glassmorphic cards and floating interfaces.
* **Metadata Fields**: Every image must define its placement, filename, alt text, prompt, and aspect ratio.
